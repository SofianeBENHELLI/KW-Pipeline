Supplier Quality Policy
Version 2.0 — Issued 2026-03-15
Owner: Quality Director, Global Operations

1. Purpose
This policy defines the minimum supplier quality requirements for materials
and components delivered to the manufacturing line. Compliance is mandatory
for every active supplier on the approved vendor list and is verified on a
continuous basis through the supplier scorecard program.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all direct-material suppliers across the approved
vendor list. Indirect-services vendors and logistics carriers are covered by
a separate procurement policy and remain out of scope for supplier quality.
Contract manufacturers are now explicitly in scope for inbound inspection.

3. Inbound Inspection
Every inbound supplier lot is sampled per AQL 1.5 acceptance criteria and the
result is recorded in the receiving inspection log. Lots that fail inbound
inspection are quarantined within eight hours and the supplier is notified
within the same shift. Repeat inspection failures within a rolling ninety-day
window trigger an automatic one-hundred-percent inspection regime.

4. Non-Conformance
A non-conformance report is opened for every quarantined supplier lot.
Suppliers have five business days to submit a containment plan and ten
business days to submit a root-cause corrective action with effectiveness
verification against the non-conformance report. The corrective action must
be reviewed by the supplier quality engineer of record.

5. Supplier Audits
Tier-1 suppliers are audited twice a year against this supplier quality
policy, alternating between on-site and remote audit modes. Tier-2 suppliers
are audited annually. Audit findings rated Major or Critical block further
purchase orders to the supplier until the corrective action has passed an
effectiveness check.

6. Records and Retention
All inbound inspection records, non-conformance reports, and supplier audit
findings are retained for ten years in the quality management system.
Records are exported nightly to the corporate archive for disaster recovery.
The quality management system remains the system of record for supplier
quality evidence requested during external regulatory audits.

7. Revisions
This supplier quality policy is reviewed every six months by the quality
director and the head of supply chain. Revisions are tracked in the quality
management system change log alongside the previous policy version.
